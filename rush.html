RUSH US!
Why rush?
rush noun, verb \ˈrəsh\
a singular, once-in-a-lifetime, Yale-unique process set up by the Yale SGC (Singing Group Council) in which talented Yalies (or Yale-affiliates) audition for any and all of the thirteen SGC a cappella groups at Yale.
What's so special about
Living Water?
Take Our Word
Looking back on my four years at Yale and the countless opportunities Yale provides, joining Living Water was the most important decision I made. I grew incredibly -- musically, personally, and spiritually. Living Water is far more than an excellent singing group with a musically diverse repertoire; it is a tight Christian community full of supportive friends with delightfully eclectic backgrounds, and it is a community that uses performance and service to reach our campus, the country and the world with the true hope of Jesus Christ. If you want to grow in your faith and be used tangibly by God during your time at Yale, and, of course, sing, Living Water provides the perfect environment to do so. - Zachary Maher '13
What do I love about living water? How close and tight-knit we are as a family. Whenever one of us is down, you really get to see God's love pour through the people surrounding you. Rehearsals are fun, the people are amazing and super friendly, and you pretty much get the best friends ever. So much love for this group! - Andres Valdivieso '16
